Frequently Asked Questions

The National Horticulture Program is an initiative aimed at promoting sustainable horticulture practices, supporting farmers, and enhancing the production and quality of fruits, vegetables, and other horticultural crops in the country.

You can participate in the National Horticulture Program by registering through the official website, attending workshops and training sessions, and applying for grants and subsidies available for horticultural projects.

The program supports a wide range of horticultural crops including fruits, vegetables, spices, medicinal plants, floriculture, and plantation crops like tea and coffee.

Yes, the National Horticulture Program offers various financial aids including subsidies, grants, and loans to support farmers and entrepreneurs in the horticulture sector.

Training and resources are provided through various channels including online courses, workshops, and agricultural extension services. You can also access educational materials and guides from the program's official website.

You can contact the National Horticulture Program's customer service through the official website or reach out to your local agricultural extension office for more personalized assistance.
